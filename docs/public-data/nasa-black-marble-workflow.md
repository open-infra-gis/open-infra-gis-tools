# NASA Black Marble / VIIRS Night-Time Lights Workflow

NASA Black Marble and VIIRS night-time lights data can be used as supporting geospatial context for environmental monitoring, disaster impact assessment, urban activity analysis, and infrastructure resilience workflows.

Night-time lights should not be treated as direct proof of outages or service conditions.

## Potential use cases

- Monitoring changes before and after natural events
- Supporting disaster impact and recovery assessment
- Understanding urban activity and settlement patterns
- Comparing light intensity with infrastructure or service areas
- Identifying areas where major changes may require further review
- Creating visual context for infrastructure resilience planning

## Generic workflow

1. Select an area of interest.
2. Access NASA Black Marble / VIIRS night-time lights data.
3. Load raster data into QGIS.
4. Reproject or align data if needed.
5. Clip data by the area of interest.
6. Compare dates or periods.
7. Symbolize change values carefully.
8. Review results with other spatial datasets.
9. Document limitations and uncertainty.

## Interpretation cautions

Night-time light changes may be affected by cloud cover, atmospheric conditions, sensor differences, seasonal effects, temporary events, snow or surface reflectance, preprocessing methods, or non-electric light sources.
